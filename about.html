About

I'm a podiatrist and developer based in Brisbane. I spend my clinical days treating patients and my evenings writing the software I wish existed when I was starting out.

This page is a slightly longer version of that story — where I work, what I'm building, and why I keep both feet in both camps.

The Clinical Side

I'm a qualified podiatrist running Principal Podiatry in Brisbane. Most of my week is patient-facing — musculoskeletal assessments, orthotic therapy, diabetic foot care, nail surgery, and the steady stream of running injuries I seem to attract (possibly karmic).

Alongside my Brisbane practice I contract to BNMAC clinics in Casino and Grafton, covering allied health needs for regional NSW communities where consistent podiatry access is genuinely hard to come by. That work keeps me honest about what rural Australia actually looks like — long days, mixed caseloads, and patients who've been waiting months for an appointment.

I trained as a clinician first. Everything I build for healthcare has that frame baked in: AHPRA compliance isn't an afterthought, patient trust isn't a growth hack, and copy that sounds like a clinician wrote it is not a coincidence.

The Software Side

I started writing code to scratch clinical itches — a better way to handle notes, a better way to help patients find good practitioners, a better way for small clinics to manage their Google reviews without tripping AHPRA. Every project I've shipped came from a problem I hit in clinic first.

Today I'm running two live SaaS products and a handful of client sites on Django, PostgreSQL, Docker Compose and DigitalOcean. I deploy manually — no over-engineered pipelines — and I keep dev and prod configurations cleanly separated. It's a small, deliberate stack that's cheap to run and easy to reason about at 10pm when something breaks.

The goal is passive-income SaaS that supports the clinical work, and vice versa: clinical work keeps me grounded in real user problems, and the software work keeps me from becoming the kind of clinician who thinks the current state of healthcare tech is acceptable.

Current Projects

  • Therapy Online Australia — Practitioner growth platform for Australian psychologists. AHPRA-verified directory with referral attribution tracking. Django, PostgreSQL, DigitalOcean.
  • TrueBlueReview — AHPRA-compliant Google review management designed specifically for registered health practitioners. Solves a compliance problem the big review platforms quietly ignore.
  • Principal Podiatry — My podiatry practice in Brisbane, plus ongoing contract work at BNMAC Casino & Grafton.
  • Medical & allied-health websites — Custom Django sites I build for clinics and consultants. See Services.
  • Marathon training — Structured sessions Wed / Thu / Sun, currently inside a 4-week block aimed at a sub-3 hour finish in April 2026.

Tools & Stack

Backend

Python, Django 4.2+, Django REST Framework, PostgreSQL in production, SQLite for dev. Gunicorn behind Nginx, Whitenoise for static files.

Infrastructure

DigitalOcean droplets, Docker Compose with separate docker-compose.dev.yml and docker-compose.prod.yml files, Nginx as reverse proxy, Let's Encrypt for TLS. Manual deploys — intentional — via docker compose up -d. Config via python-decouple and .env files, never inline secrets.

Frontend

Hand-written HTML and CSS, vanilla JS for anything interactive, progressive enhancement by default. GSAP when animation earns its keep. No framework tax.

Workflow

Git, feature branches, never to main. PRs via gh CLI. Tests for anything touching billing, auth, or data integrity. Claude Code as a daily driver for review, refactor and grunt work.

Running

Distance running is the third leg of the stool. I'm inside a structured block chasing a sub-3 hour marathon — Wednesday intervals, Thursday tempo, Sunday long run. It's where I work out the problems I can't solve at a desk, and it's a useful reminder that consistent, unglamorous effort compounds in both training and business.
