What I Build

Modern Django sites

Hand-built on Django 4+, PostgreSQL and Docker. Fast, maintainable, and designed to outlive whatever page-builder fad is dominating this quarter. Server-rendered, SEO-friendly, genuinely accessible.

Mobile-first design

Most of your patients will first meet your clinic on a phone. Every site I build is designed for that phone first, then scaled up to desktop — not the other way around.

AHPRA-aware copy

I write website copy that sounds like a clinician wrote it, because a clinician did. No "world-class specialists," no fabricated testimonials, no language that'll get flagged in an ad-code review.

Fast, reliable hosting

Deployed on DigitalOcean with Docker, Nginx, and Let's Encrypt. No shared hosting, no WordPress surprises at 2am. You get a dedicated environment that's cheap to run and easy to back up.

AI Operating System Installs

A different kind of work, for clinics that already have a website but are drowning in admin. I install a working AI assistant inside your practice — wired into your accounting, calendar, inbox, drive and patient management software — so the boring half of running a clinic gets quietly handled in the background.

I've been running this in my own podiatry practice for months. Receipts auto-reconcile against the bank feed in Xero. End-of-day summaries land on my phone. The system has memory of how the practice runs and sounds like the practice when it drafts replies. I install the same stack for other clinic owners and stay on retainer to keep adding to it.

What's in the Install

Practice memory

The AI gets loaded with how your clinic actually works — fee schedule, treatment protocols, brand voice, AHPRA boundaries, common questions. It stops sounding like ChatGPT and starts sounding like your practice.

Real integrations

Wired into Xero (or MYOB), your PMS — Cliniko, Nookal, Halaxy, Power Diary — Gmail/Outlook, Google Drive, calendar, SMS, and Stripe if you take card payments. Not a screenshot demo. Live data.

Working automations on day one

You leave the install with three things already running: photo-a-receipt → expense in Xero, no-show follow-up SMS drafts, and a daily phone summary of the day's revenue, tomorrow's bookings and anything urgent.

A chat channel you actually use

Talk to the system in plain language from your phone — Discord, Telegram or SMS, whichever you prefer. "What did we bill last week?" "Draft a recall message for everyone overdue on orthotic reviews." It just answers.

Why This, Specifically From Me

Every AI agency right now is selling automations top-down: scope a build, deliver one workflow, repeat. I'm selling the bottom-up version — install a single contextualised assistant first, then layer everything else on top. It's faster, cheaper, and the workflows are far better because every one of them starts with full context of your practice.

The credibility part is simple: I'm a registered podiatrist running this exact stack in my own clinic in Brisbane. You can see it working before you commit to anything. No theoretical case studies, no carousel of stock-photo dashboards.

The 5-Day Install

Day 1 — Discovery

Understand the practice

I sit with you and the front desk for the day. I learn how the clinic actually runs, where the time leaks are, and what you'd happily never do again. I leave with a punch list.

Day 2 — Setup

Stand up the OS

The AI box and supporting infrastructure get deployed (in your clinic, on your hardware, or on a private server). Your practice context is loaded and the integrations get wired up one by one.

Day 3 — First automations

Three workflows live

Receipt capture into Xero. No-show follow-up drafts. End-of-day summary to your phone. Before close of business on day three, all three are running on real data.

Day 4 — Training

Hands on the steering wheel

You and the front desk learn how to talk to the system from a chat channel. We do real tasks together. By the end of the day, the team is using it without me in the room.

Day 5 — Stress test

Run it like a Friday

The system handles a normal busy day with me observing. Anything that breaks gets fixed. We agree the first month's roadmap, then I leave you with a working stack and a calendar invite for the first follow-up.
